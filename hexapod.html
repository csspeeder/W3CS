Project Case Study

Hexapod Robot

A six-legged robotic prototype built around custom 3D-printed mechanics, 18 servo actuators, inverse kinematics and embedded control.

Back to Projects

Goal

Build a functional walking platform.

The aim was not a static CAD model. The robot had to move as a real mechanical system: six legs, three degrees of freedom per leg, controlled gait patterns and enough structural stiffness to survive testing.

Mechanical Design

3D-printed structure with modular legs.

The platform uses printed body and leg components with separate coxa, femur and tibia segments. The geometry was designed around reach, ground clearance, servo placement and printable part strength.

Control

Inverse kinematics and gait logic.

Each foot position is converted into joint angles through inverse kinematics. Tripod, ripple and wave-style movement concepts can be tested as different compromises between speed, stability and smoothness.

System

Hardware, power and iteration.

The prototype combines embedded control, servo actuation, battery power and a custom control workflow. The difficult part is not one component; it is making mechanics, electronics and software behave as one system.
